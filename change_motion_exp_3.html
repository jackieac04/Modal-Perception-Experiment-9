Object Experiment

Welcome! Thank you for participating in our study.

Important, before you begin:

Please do this experiment on a laptop or desktop computer. If you are viewing this experiment on a phone or a tablet, please close the window and return later with non-mobile device.

Second: please maximize the window before you continue.

Click 'continue' once you have maximized the window and are ready to move on.

In this experiment, every animation will present one disk to the left of either one or two centrally located rectangles (occluders). During "preview," one shape will appear on the disk. After 1.2 seconds, the shape will disappear and the disk will move behind the occluder(s). If there are two occluders, it will clearly move behind one of them, however if there is just one occluder, the disk will move ambiguously behind the middle of the occluder. If there is just one occluder, it will split into two occluders. Both occluders will then move off-screen revealing the location of the disk. The disk will either be in the location you expect it, or it will appear in the opposite location you expect. After a moment, a shape will be visible on the disk. This shape may be the same or different than the original shape displayed in the preview.

If the shape is the same as in the preview, press 1 to indicate they are the same. If the shape is replaced by a new shape, press 2 to indicate there is a new shape. It does not matter if the disks reappear in the original locations. The only thing that matters is that the shape on the disk at the end of the animation is the same as the shape at the beginning of the animation. In short, you should base your decision solely on whether the preview shape is the same as the final shape.

Click 'Practice' so you can try it!

When the shapes reappear:

Press 1 if the shape is the same at the end and the beginning of the trial.

Press 2 if the shape at the end of the trial is different.

Do this as quickly as possible, while remaining accurate!

The practice is now complete. The experiment is about to start. The experiment will take around 40 minutes.

Click 'Start' when you are ready. Remember, press 1 or 2 on the keyboard as fast as possible to indicate that the shape is the same as you saw before the disk moved (1), or there is a new shape (2).

click 'next' to continue.

The experiment is finished. Please click 'submit' to submit your data. Thank you!
